General Overview

Design docs are informal planning documents used before implementation to align a team on what’s being built, why it matters, and how it will work. They prioritize scope, constraints, and tradeoffs over deep technical detail.

A strong design doc makes goals/non-goals explicit, surfaces risks early, and justifies a chosen approach while acknowledging alternatives. The audience often includes engineers, technical leads, and stakeholders who need a clear overview to approve or critique the plan.
