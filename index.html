Bio

I’m a Computer Engineering student who treats writing like an engineering tool: it should make complex work easier to understand, use, and trust. My approach is reader-centered—start with a clear purpose, anticipate what the reader needs, choose an appropriate tone, and structure information so a busy audience can scan, understand, and act confidently.

What this site is

A correspondence portfolio demonstrating professional writing skills (not a résumé site).

What you’ll see

Three workplace genres + explainers connecting choices to reader-centered principles.

Reader-centered writing focus

Rhetorical situation

  • Audience + purpose first
  • Genre that fits the context
  • Credible, appropriate tone

Usability

  • Clear headings + scannable structure
  • Specific, actionable content
  • Revision for clarity + concision
