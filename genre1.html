General Overview

Technical documentation in the computer hardware industry is typically formal, highly structured, and detail-oriented. It is written for engineers and developers who already have domain background, so it uses industry terminology (e.g., GPIO, power rails, PCB, DAC) and prioritizes precision over tone.

These documents are designed to be skimmable and non-linear—readers jump to the section they need—so authors rely on headings, diagrams, tables, checklists, and short blocks of text to prevent ambiguity.
