General Overview

Change logs (and revision notes) are chronological records of notable updates made between versions of a project. They’re built for scanning: short bullets, clear headings (Added/Changed/Fixed), and minimal fluff.

The primary audience is typically developers/contributors (and sometimes end users), so the writing often uses technical terms but doesn’t assume the reader needs deep explanation for every change—just what changed and why it matters.
